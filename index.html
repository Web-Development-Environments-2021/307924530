About

Grandpa Joe was born in New York City in November 7th, 1922, when it was still filled with sand and there were only a few houses built.
Their neighbors were mostly Native Americans, and grandpa Joe told us how his sister and him would walk afoot to their farm to get milk and eggs.
Most of his childhood he spent studying, as he was an intelectually gifted young man, and even skipped 2 grades :)
After high school he joined the US Air Force and fought in World War 2. After the war he studied in Columbia University, NYC.
Grandma Rachel was born in Bucharest, Romania in August 3rd, 1928. When she was 10 years old, her family's entire possessions were taken by the Nazis
and they all had to go underground and live in fear.
At age 15, grandma Rachel moved to Israel on her own. She lived in an agriculture boarding school and had a hard time there.
She later joined the "Lehaka Tzvai'it" (military vocal group) in the Israeli Defense Forces, and after her service moved to Tel-Aviv.
Grandma Rachel always knew she wanted to be an actress, and Tel-Aviv was her place to start. She became an actress in the Habima Theater and even changed her
last name to Timor, a name that was suggested to her by the poet Nathan Alterman fitting her "tamir" (upright, tall) looks. In 1956, grandma Rachel was visiting the artist village Ein Hod.
Grandpa Joe had arrived in Israel as a tourist and was also
visiting the same place. The two met and only one month later they got married and moved to NYC.
There Grandpa Joe was a teacher and Grandma Rachel studied acting. My mom and her two sisters were born during the time in NYC.
Grandpa Joe was a real book worm. He would finish 400 pagers overnight. He loved art, traveling, and would spend a lot of time at the sea.
He was definitely a nonconformist at his time:
He had long hair, would go to work wearing jeans and sandals, and he never learned Hebrew even though he was a lecturer at the Hebrew University for most of his life.
Grandma Rachel was an artist in her body and soul, she had a special joie de vivre and was always creating.
Cheers guys, miss you.

Q & A

Question Number Web 1.0 Web 2.0 Web 3.0
Q1 Web 1.0 is read-only and static-
users can only read the contents of a web page. There is no interaction between users.		 Web 2.0 is read-write-
users could write and read web pages, and it was more about communication. Web 2.0 was responsive to user input and contained dynamic content.		 Web 3.0 is portable and personal-
The read-write operations are more intelligent. AI is introduced to the web and it learns what the user wants and delivers a Personalized web experience.
In Web 1.0 search engines retrieve macro contents, therefore the search is very fast but the results are inaccurate or more than users can process. In Web 2.0 search engines retrieve tags with micro contents. Since the process of tagging is done manually, it covers negligible percents of the World Wide Web. The search is not as fast as in Web 1.0 but it's much more accurate. In Web 3.0 search engines will retrieve micro content texts which were tagged automatically. The macro contents of Web 1.0 needs to be translated into micro contents so that the search results will become more precise.

Q2 Example Query: "How many seasons in Seinfeld?"
The user must type in the requested URL into the search engine i.e. the users needs to know the exact address of the page he is looking for The user can type in the actual query into the search engine. The user gets a list of web pages that used appropriate tags to associate with the query. The user starts to type in the actual query and the search engine suggests auto-completion. The user gets a list of web pages that are semantically related to the search query and are suited to the user's specific history, location, age, ETC.
